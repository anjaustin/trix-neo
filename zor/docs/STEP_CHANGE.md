### Tagline
**"From frozen shapes to frozen silicon"**

### The Vision

#### 1. Software Is 100-1000x Inefficient

**Current state:**
- TriX on ARM Cortex-M: ~50M inferences/sec, 10-100mW
- General-purpose CPU: Optimized for arbitrary code
- Memory transfers: Most energy spent moving data
- Instruction fetch: Wastes cycles decoding

**With custom silicon:**
- 5 Primes implemented in hardware units
- Shapes execute in dedicated pipelines
- No instruction fetch (dataflow architecture)
- No memory transfers (on-chip SRAM)

**Expected improvement:**
- 100-1000x lower power (10µW-100µW vs 10mW)
- 10-100x higher throughput (500M-5B ops/sec)
- 10x smaller die area (<0.5mm² vs 5mm²)

## Step-Change Option 3: The Theoretical Play 🎓

### Tagline
**"The periodic table of intelligence"**

### The Vision
Prove that the **5 Primes are actually minimal and complete** for differentiable computation. Make TriX a mathematical theory, not just an engineering artifact.

---

### Why This Works

#### 1. TriX Lacks Academic Legitimacy

**Current state:**
- No peer-reviewed publications
- No formal proofs
- Claims are empirical, not theoretical
- Academic community unaware

**With theoretical foundation:**
- Peer-reviewed papers at NeurIPS/ICML/PLDI
- Formal proofs of minimality and completeness
- Academic citations and credibility
- Textbook-worthy contribution

#### 2. The 5 Primes Claim Needs Proof

**Current claim:**
> "All differentiable computation can be built from {ADD, MUL, EXP, MAX, CONST}"

**Status:** Asserted, not proven.

**What's needed:**
1. **Minimality:** Prove you can't remove any of the 5
2. **Completeness:** Prove all differentiable functions decompose
3. **Efficiency:** Prove bounds on composition depth
4. **Uniqueness:** Prove no other minimal set exists

**This would be a foundational contribution** like:
- Turing machines for computability
- Lambda calculus for functional programming
- Boolean algebra for digital logic

#### 3. Academic Impact Enables Ecosystem

**With academic legitimacy:**
- PhD students work on TriX
- Research groups extend the theory
- Courses teach frozen computation
- Textbooks include TriX
- Standards bodies adopt paradigm

**Long-term impact:**
- TriX becomes foundational knowledge
- Cited for decades
- Shapes how next generation thinks about AI
- Platform for entire research community

### The Strategy

#### Phase 1: Formalization (Months 1-6)

**Goal:** Translate intuition into formal mathematics

**Step 1: Define the Model**
- Formal definition of frozen shapes
- Formal definition of 5 Primes
- Composition rules (how shapes combine)
- Equivalence classes (when are two shapes the same?)

**Step 2: State the Theorems**
- **Theorem 1 (Completeness):** Every differentiable function f: ℝⁿ → ℝᵐ can be approximated to arbitrary precision by compositions of the 5 Primes.
- **Theorem 2 (Minimality):** No proper subset of {ADD, MUL, EXP, MAX, CONST} is complete.
- **Theorem 3 (Efficiency):** For any function f with Kolmogorov complexity K(f), the shortest 5-Prime decomposition has depth O(K(f)).
- **Theorem 4 (Uniqueness):** Any other minimal complete set is isomorphic to the 5 Primes.

**Step 3: Initial Proofs**
- Prove completeness for simple cases (polynomials, ReLU networks)
- Prove minimality by contradiction (removal breaks completeness)
- Establish complexity bounds for common functions
