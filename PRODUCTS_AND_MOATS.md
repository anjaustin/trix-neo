## Competitive Strategy

### The Four Strategic Principles

**Principle 1: Don't compete on code quality**

❌ **Wrong approach:** "Our framework is better engineered"  
✅ **Right approach:** "Our framework is the only one certified"

**Why:** Code quality is infinite competition (always someone better). Certification is binary (you have it or you don't).

**Implication:** Open-source everything. Let competitors copy the code. They still can't copy the FDA approval.

---

**Principle 2: Don't compete on performance**

❌ **Wrong approach:** "We're 10% faster than TensorFlow"  
✅ **Right approach:** "We're the only framework certified for medical devices"

**Why:** Performance is a race to the bottom (marginal improvements). Certification is a step function (enables entire markets).

**Implication:** 235 GOP/s is "good enough." Focus on certification, not optimization.

---

**Principle 3: Don't compete on features**

❌ **Wrong approach:** "We support 50 neural network architectures"  
✅ **Right approach:** "We support the 3 architectures FDA approves"

**Why:** Features are infinite (always more to add). Regulatory requirements are finite (fixed list).

**Implication:** Build narrow, deep. Do deterministic AI perfectly, not general AI broadly.

---

**Principle 4: Do compete on moats**

✅ **Right approach:** "Get FDA approval before competitors even start"

**Why:** Moats compound. First-mover advantage is permanent in regulatory domains.

**Implication:** Race to FDA approval, not to GitHub stars. Market presence > code quality.

---
